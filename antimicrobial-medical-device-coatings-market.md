Thromboresistant coatings reduce blood clot formation on devices, enhancing their safety and efficacy in vascular applications. Hydrophilic coatings improve biocompatibility by attracting water, reducing friction and tissue adhesion, which is crucial for devices like catheters. Other coatings may include antimicrobial agents to prevent infection. Each type addresses specific challenges in medical device performance and patient outcomes, driving innovation in the healthcare industry.</p></p>
