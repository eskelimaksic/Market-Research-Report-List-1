<p><p>Breast cancer therapeutics encompass various drug categories targeting specific cancer cell functions. Mitotic inhibitors disrupt cell division, halting tumor growth. Anti-metabolites interfere with DNA synthesis, preventing cancer cells from multiplying. Hormone receptor therapies block hormones that fuel certain breast cancers. Aromatase inhibitors reduce estrogen production, particularly in postmenopausal women. HER2 inhibitors target the HER2 protein overexpressed in some breast cancers, slowing their progression. Together, these therapies enhance treatment outcomes and improve survival rates for patients.</p></p>
